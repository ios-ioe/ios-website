About IOS

IOE Pulchowk Campus student community—research before code, impact before hype.

Who we are

Welcome to Ignite Open Source (IOS)—the first dedicated open-source community at Institute of Engineering, Pulchowk Campus. We combine research-driven development with a mission to tackle real problems in Nepal: structured discovery first, then solutions we publish for everyone.

We prioritize AI for social good and ethical open-source development across healthcare, education, government transparency, and agriculture—building in the open so communities can adopt, extend, and trust what we ship.

What we do

How IOS turns research into public goods—not slide decks.

Research-driven

We identify Nepal-specific problems through structured research before we write code—so solutions match reality on the ground.

Open-source first

Tools are shared publicly on GitHub and PyPI so anyone can use, audit, extend, and remix what we build.

Interdisciplinary

Open to every department—collaborative, non-political, and non-profit.

Solution-oriented

A full pipeline: problem research → solution development → sharing with the wider community.

Domains of focus

We prioritize ethical open-source development and AI for social good in sectors that matter for Nepal.

Healthcare

  • Accessible diagnostics
  • Patient data management
  • Rural health monitoring

Education

  • Adaptive learning platforms
  • Resource-sharing tools for underserved areas

Government transparency

  • Open data tools
  • Civic engagement platforms

Agriculture

  • Farmer advisory apps
  • Environmental monitoring

Our objectives

Core work we optimize for—and supportive programs that grow the ecosystem.

Primary — core focus

Research
Thorough research on the Nepali context to find problems where open-source solutions can make a tangible difference.
Build
High-quality, open-source tools built through active student collaboration—directly addressing those problems.

Secondary — supportive

  • Events — Hackathons, workshops, and collaborative events for innovation and teamwork.
  • Awareness — Programs on what open source can do for Nepal.
  • Global contributions — Helping members contribute to international OSS projects.
  • Education — Resources for beginners: coding fundamentals and first contributions.

Benefits

Why students show up—and what Nepal gains when we ship in the open.

For students

  • Real-world, portfolio-building experience: research, Git workflows, documentation, deployment.
  • Leadership, teamwork, and soft skills through roles and events.
  • Mentorship and networking with faculty, alumni, industry, and global OSS communities.
  • Lower barriers for beginners—guided paths into coding and first contributions.

For Nepal

  • Free, accessible solutions tailored to local challenges.
  • A stronger national tech ecosystem—engineers who contribute locally and globally.
  • Digital innovation without reliance on costly proprietary software.
